LEGEND

In legends, Hummingbird is often portrayed as a healer or as a spirit being who helps people in need, and sometimes plays the important mythological role of fire-bringer. Seeing a hummingbird is a sign of good luck in many Native American tribes. In ancient Mexico, hummingbirds were considered sacred and associated with royalty and warriors; the Aztec god Huitzilopochtli, had the hummingbird as his divine animal and was sometimes depicted in hummingbird form in traditional Aztec art. In some Mexican tribes today, hummingbirds are believed to be messengers from the afterworld or manifestations of a dead person's spirit.

FACTS

The only birds that can fly up, down, sideways, forwards, and, backwards!

The hummingbird family name, Trochilidae, derives from the Greek word that Aristotle used for a small bird.

Their brains make up 4.2% of their body weight. Proportionally speaking, they have the largest brains of the wild bird group.

Hummingbirds see in ultraviolet light and they can see further than a human. It has a dense concentration of cones in their retinas. These cones contain pigments and oil droplets in shades of yellow to red, which seem to act like filters. The filters appear to heighten color sensitivity to red and also yellow, while muting colors such as blue.
